# Cold Email Formulas & Examples

Proven formula breakdowns and annotated examples across industries and use cases.

---

## The PPPP Formula (Problem → Promise → Proof → Proposal)

Best for: warm-ish cold outreach where you've identified a clear pain point.

```
[PROBLEM] — name the pain they have, in their language
[PROMISE] — what changes when they work with you
[PROOF] — one data point or customer example
[PROPOSAL] — single, low-friction CTA
```

**Example:**
```
Subject: [Company]'s reporting workflow

Hi Sarah,

Most marketing directors at Series B SaaS companies are still pulling weekly reports manually — 
averaging 4+ hours a week that could be spent on strategy.
[PROBLEM]

We built [Product] to cut that to 15 minutes, fully automated.
[PROMISE]

[Customer] went from 6 hours to 12 minutes in their first week.
[PROOF]

Worth a quick look? Happy to show you a live demo of your metrics in 15 minutes.
[PROPOSAL]

— James
```

## The AIDA Formula (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action)

Best for: campaigns where you're leading with insight or a provocative angle.

```
[ATTENTION] — hook with a surprising stat, question, or observation
[INTEREST] — make them care about the hook
[DESIRE] — show the outcome they could have
[ACTION] — CTA
```

**Example:**
```
Subject: 68% of SaaS trials never activate

Hi Tom,

68% of free trial signups never use a product's core feature.
[ATTENTION]

For a product like [Company]'s, that's potentially hundreds of trials a month converting below their potential.
[INTEREST]

Teams we work with typically recover 15–20% of those trials with targeted onboarding sequences — 
without changing the product at all.
[DESIRE]

Is activation on your radar for this quarter?
[ACTION]

— Alex
```

## The Before/After Formula

Best for: concrete, outcome-focused outreach with strong before/after data.

```
[Before state — the pain they're in now]
[After state — what their world looks like with your solution]
[How you get them there]
[CTA]
```

**Example:**
```
Subject: before/after — [Company]'s content ROI

Hi Priya,

Before working with us: content team publishing 3x/week, 
zero visibility into what's driving pipeline.

After: same team, same output — but now every piece ties 
to a revenue metric. Closed $340K in influenced pipeline last quarter.

We do this by connecting [their current tool] to the metrics 
that actually matter. Takes about 2 weeks to set up.

Worth 20 minutes to see if it applies to [Company]?

— Dan
```

## The Insight Lead

Best for: high-value accounts, founder-to-founder, exec outreach. Leads with a genuine insight rather than a pitch.

```
[Specific, non-obvious insight about their industry/situation]
[Why this matters to them]
[Brief intro to who you are and what you do]
[CTA — offer to share more, not to sell]
```

**Example:**
```
Subject: something we noticed in your category

Hi Marcus,

SaaS companies that launch a PLG motion after Series A tend to underinvest 
in the activation layer — the product does the selling, but nobody 
optimises the first 72 hours.

We've seen this pattern a lot working with [Customer A] and [Customer B] 
through their growth stages.

I run [Company] — we specialise in exactly this problem.

Happy to share what's worked if useful. No pitch, just the data.

— Raj
```

## The Referral/Warm Intro Email

When you have a mutual connection or were referred:

```
Subject: [Mutual name] suggested I reach out

Hi [Name],

[Mutual] mentioned you're the right person to talk to about [topic] at [Company].

[Brief intro — who you are, what you do, for who]

[Mutual] thought there might be a fit given [specific reason].

Worth a quick chat to see?

— [Name]
```

## The Competitor Switch Email

When you know they're using a competitor:

```
Subject: [Company] + [competitor] → [your product]?

Hi [Name],

Noticed [Company] is using [competitor] for [use case]. A lot of teams come to us 
when they've outgrown [competitor] — usually when [specific pain point with competitor].

We've helped [similar company] make the switch in [timeframe] with no data loss.

Is that pain point showing up for your team yet?

— [Name]
```

Use with caution — only if you genuinely know they're using the competitor (LinkedIn tech stack tools, job posting language, etc.).

## Subject Line Swipe File

### Personalised
- "[Company]'s [metric / problem]"
- "question for [Company]'s [their title]"
- "saw your post on [topic]"
- "[their first name] — quick thought"
- "[Company] + [what you do]"

### Outcome-Focused
- "more [desired outcome] from [channel]"
- "[timeframe] to [specific result]"
- "how [similar company] [achieved result]"

### Curiosity
- "handling [pain point] manually still?"
- "something we noticed in [industry]"
- "quick question about [their product/thing]"
- "[Stat that will surprise them]"

### Trigger Event
- "congrats on [funding/launch/milestone]"
- "re: [Company]'s [job posting]"
- "saw [Company] just [did thing]"

## Reply Rate Benchmarks

Use these as rough orientation, not targets. Vary heavily by industry, ICP, and list quality.

| Sequence Type | Cold (no prior contact) | Warm (triggered) |
|---|---|---|
| Generic blast | 0.5–2% | — |
| ICP-targeted, Tier 3 personalisation | 3–6% | — |
| ICP-targeted, Tier 2 personalisation | 5–10% | — |
| 1:1 Tier 1 personalisation | 10–20% | — |
| Trigger-event email | — | 15–30% |
| Referral/warm intro | — | 25–40% |

If you're below the bottom of the range: fix relevance and personalisation first, then copy.
If you're in range but want more: test subject lines and CTAs.

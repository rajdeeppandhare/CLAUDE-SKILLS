# Keyword Strategy Guide

How to find, evaluate, and prioritise keywords for maximum organic growth.

---

## The Keyword Research Process

### Step 1: Build Your Seed List

Start with these sources:

**From your product:**
- Every feature, use case, and problem you solve
- How your ICP describes their problem (use VOC research — see copywriting skill)
- Your product's category name + adjacent categories

**From your customers:**
- What words did they type to find you? (check Search Console "Queries")
- What did they Google before they knew your product existed?
- What terms appear in their support requests?

**From competitors:**
- What keywords are they ranking for? (Ahrefs, Semrush Site Explorer)
- What pages get them the most traffic?

**From Google:**
- Google autocomplete (type your seed keyword, see suggestions)
- "People also ask" boxes
- Related searches at the bottom of SERP
- Search Console for terms you already rank for (but could rank higher)

## Step 2: Classify by Intent

Every keyword belongs to one intent type. Map it before targeting it.

### Informational Intent
**What:** User wants to learn something
**Keywords:** "how to," "what is," "guide to," "examples of," "why does"
**Best page type:** Blog post, guide, resource
**Funnel stage:** Top of funnel (awareness)

### Commercial Investigation Intent
**What:** User is evaluating options before buying
**Keywords:** "best [tool] for [use case]," "[tool] vs [tool]," "[tool] alternatives," "[tool] review," "[tool] pricing"
**Best page type:** Comparison page, alternatives page, review-style landing page
**Funnel stage:** Middle of funnel (consideration)

### Transactional Intent
**What:** User is ready to act
**Keywords:** "[tool] free trial," "buy [product]," "[tool] sign up," "[tool] demo"
**Best page type:** Homepage, product page, pricing page
**Funnel stage:** Bottom of funnel (decision)

### Navigational Intent
**What:** User wants to find a specific brand or site
**Keywords:** "[Brand] login," "[Brand] pricing," "[Brand] support"
**Best page type:** The page they're looking for — make sure it's indexed and findable

## Step 3: Evaluate Each Keyword

Score every keyword on four dimensions:

### Volume
Monthly search volume (from Ahrefs, Semrush, or Google Keyword Planner).

| Volume | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 10,000+ | High volume — competitive, broader intent |
| 1,000–10,000 | Medium volume — good balance of traffic and specificity |
| 100–1,000 | Lower volume — often high intent, less competition |
| < 100 | Niche — can still convert if highly relevant |

Don't chase volume at the expense of relevance. A 200-search keyword where you can rank #1 and convert 10% beats a 10,000-search keyword where you rank #15 and convert 0.1%.

### Keyword Difficulty
How hard is it to rank on page 1?

| KD Score (Ahrefs) | What It Means | Good for |
|---|---|---|
| 0–20 | Low — realistic for new or small sites | Any site |
| 20–40 | Medium — need some authority | Sites with some backlinks |
| 40–60 | Hard — need good authority + great content | Established sites |
| 60–80 | Very hard — competitive, high DR sites dominate | High DR sites only |
| 80+ | Extremely hard — national/global brands | Enterprise only |

**Reality check:** Look at the actual ranking pages, not just the score. If the top 10 are all Wikipedia, Forbes, and HubSpot — avoid. If smaller sites are ranking, you have a shot.

### Relevance
How closely does this keyword map to something your product directly solves?

- **High relevance:** Keyword describes your product's core use case
- **Medium relevance:** Keyword is related to your ICP's problems but not directly about your product
- **Low relevance:** Adjacent topic — can drive traffic but may not convert

Prioritise high relevance, even at lower volume.

### Business Value
If you rank for this, does it drive pipeline?

- **High value:** Transactional and commercial keywords, closely related to your product
- **Medium value:** Informational keywords where your ICP is searching — good for awareness and retargeting
- **Low value:** High volume, low relevance — traffic that won't convert

## Step 4: Prioritise

Use this scoring matrix to rank keywords:

| Factor | Weight | Score (1–5) | Weighted Score |
|---|---|---|---|
| Relevance | 30% | | |
| Business value | 25% | | |
| Volume | 20% | | |
| Difficulty (inverse — lower = higher score) | 25% | | |

**Total score = sum of weighted scores**

Highest-scoring keywords are your top priorities.

## Topic Clusters

Build authority by covering a topic deeply, not just targeting isolated keywords.

**Pillar page:** Broad, comprehensive page covering the core topic
- Example: "Project Management for Remote Teams — Complete Guide"
- Target keyword: "project management remote teams"

**Cluster pages:** Narrower posts covering subtopics, all internally linking to the pillar
- "How to Run a Standup Meeting Remotely"
- "Best Tools for Remote Team Collaboration"
- "How to Measure Remote Team Productivity"

The cluster pages reinforce the pillar's authority, and the pillar provides context for the cluster pages. Together they rank better than isolated pages.

## Content Gap Analysis

Find keywords your competitors rank for that you don't:

1. In Ahrefs → Site Explorer → enter competitor URL
2. Go to "Organic Keywords" → export
3. Filter for keywords ranking in positions 1–20
4. Remove keywords you already target
5. Identify the highest-relevance, lowest-difficulty gaps

Do this for 3–5 competitors. The keywords appearing across multiple competitors are the highest-priority gaps.

## SERP Feature Targeting

Beyond organic rankings, target these SERP features:

| Feature | How to Target |
|---|---|
| Featured Snippet | Answer a question directly in 40–60 words, use clear headings |
| People Also Ask | Include exact question as an H2 or H3, answer immediately below |
| Local Pack | Google Business Profile, local schema, local citations |
| Image Pack | High-quality images, descriptive file names, alt text |
| Video Carousel | YouTube videos optimised with title/description keywords |
| Sitelinks | Strong internal linking, clear site structure |

---

## Keyword Tracking

Once you're targeting keywords, track:
- Current ranking position (Ahrefs Rank Tracker, Semrush Position Tracking)
- Ranking movement week-over-week
- Clicks and impressions from Search Console
- Conversion rate from organic traffic to that page

Review monthly. Pages that gain traction → invest more. Pages stuck below position 20 after 6 months → reassess content quality or try a different angle.

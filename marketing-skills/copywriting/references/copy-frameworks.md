# Copy Frameworks & Headline Formulas

Reference guide for proven copywriting structures, headline patterns, and page templates.

---

## Headline Formulas

### Outcome-Based
- "{Achieve outcome} without {pain point}"
- "The fastest way to {desired result}"
- "Go from {current state} to {desired state} in {timeframe}"
- "{Number} {audience} use {product} to {outcome}"

### Problem-Based
- "Tired of {pain point}?"
- "Stop {undesirable activity}"
- "Never {unpleasant event} again"
- "Why is {common thing} still so hard?"

### Category Definition
- "The {adjective} {category} for {audience}"
- "{Product} for {specific audience} who {do thing}"
- "The only {category} that {key differentiator}"

### Curiosity/Question
- "{Question that highlights main pain}?"
- "What if {desirable scenario}?"
- "How {company/person} {achieved result} without {sacrifice}"

### Social Proof
- "Join {number} {audience} who {outcome}"
- "The {category} trusted by {credible group}"

## Subheadline Patterns

The subheadline expands on the headline and adds specificity. Always answers: *who it's for*, *what it does*, and *why it matters*.

Pattern: `[Product] [does X] so [audience] can [outcome] — without [pain].`

Examples:
- "Notion replaces your scattered notes, docs, and wikis with one connected workspace."
- "Loom replaces slow meetings with fast async video — so you can move faster and get your time back."

## Section Templates

### Problem Section
1. Name the problem (use customer language)
2. Describe what it feels like (empathy)
3. List the consequences of not solving it
4. Transition: "There's a better way."

### Solution/Benefits Section
- Lead with the outcome, not the feature
- Use 3–5 benefits max (more = confusion)
- Each benefit = headline + 1–2 sentences
- Format: Icon + Benefit Headline + Short Body

### How It Works (3-step pattern)
1. [First action] — [short description]
2. [Second action] — [short description]
3. [Result] — [short description]

### Social Proof Patterns
- **Logo bar**: "Trusted by teams at [Company A], [Company B]..."
- **Stat callouts**: "[X]% of users [outcome]. [Y]K teams switched from [competitor]."
- **Testimonial format**: Quote → Name, Title, Company → optional metric result

### Objection Handling (FAQ) Patterns
- "Is this right for me if I'm [edge case]?"
- "How long does [thing] take?"
- "What happens if I [worst case]?"
- "How is this different from [competitor]?"
- "What if I want to cancel?"

### Final CTA Section
1. Restate the core value promise (1 sentence)
2. Remove remaining risk (guarantee, free trial, no CC)
3. CTA button
4. Optional: secondary CTA for not-ready visitors

## Natural Transition Phrases

Use between sections to maintain flow and logical argument:

**Setting up the problem:**
- "Here's the thing..."
- "But here's what actually happens..."
- "Sound familiar?"
- "It doesn't have to be this way."

**Introducing the solution:**
- "That's exactly why we built [Product]."
- "Meet [Product] — the [category] that [outcome]."
- "There's a better way."
- "Introducing [Product]."

**Moving to proof:**
- "Don't just take our word for it."
- "See what [audience] are saying."
- "Here's what happens when you use [Product]."

**Closing/CTA:**
- "Ready to [outcome]?"
- "Start [doing thing] today."
- "See it for yourself."
- "Your [outcome] starts here."

## Voice of Customer Mining

Pull language directly from:
- G2 / Capterra / Trustpilot reviews (especially 4-star — specific and honest)
- Reddit threads where your ICP vents about the problem
- Support ticket language
- Sales call transcripts ("I was frustrated with...", "I needed...")
- Twitter/X complaints about category problems

Look for:
- Exact phrases they use to describe the pain
- The words they use for desired outcomes
- Metaphors they reach for
- What they compared you to before choosing

## Copy Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Example | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Buzzword soup | "Leverage synergies to optimise workflows" | Name the actual thing |
| Feature-first | "We have 200+ integrations" | "Connect the tools you already use" |
| Passive voice | "Reports are generated automatically" | "We generate reports automatically" |
| Weak CTA | "Submit" / "Learn More" | "Get My Free Report" |
| Over-qualified | "We can almost always help you save time" | "Cut reporting from 4h to 15min" |
| Generic headline | "The best platform for your business" | "The project tool that PMs actually use" |
| Wall of text hero | 3 paragraphs above the fold | One headline. One subhead. One CTA. |

# Voice of Customer (VOC) Research Guide

How to find, extract, and use real customer language in your copy.

---

## Why VOC Matters

The best copy doesn't sound like marketing. It sounds like your customer describing their own problem — and your product answering it. VOC research closes that gap. When someone reads copy built from their own words, they think: *"This is exactly my situation."*

---

## Source Tier 1: High-Signal (Use First)

### Review Sites
**Where:** G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, App Store, Product Hunt

**What to pull:**
- 4-star reviews (honest, specific — not gushing, not furious)
- 1–2 star reviews of competitors (their pain = your opportunity)
- Phrases customers use to describe outcomes ("I finally feel in control of...")
- Before/after language ("Before [product], I used to... Now I can...")

**Search operators:**
- `site:g2.com "[competitor name]" reviews`
- `site:capterra.com "[category]" reviews`

---

### Reddit
**Where:** Subreddits where your ICP lives (r/entrepreneur, r/SaaS, r/marketing, r/smallbusiness, etc.)

**What to pull:**
- Rant posts about the problem you solve ("I hate how hard it is to...")
- "What tool do you use for X?" threads — read why people switch
- Upvoted comments describing frustrations with category

**Search operators:**
- `site:reddit.com "[pain point]" OR "[category]" [keyword]`
- Use Reddit search with "Top" filter + past year

---

### Sales Call Transcripts / Support Tickets
**Where:** Your own Gong, Chorus, Intercom, Zendesk, etc.

**What to pull:**
- How do prospects describe their problem before they know your product?
- What words do they use for "urgent" vs "nice to have"?
- What comparison do they make? ("I've been using [X], but...")
- What outcome do they actually want? (not what they say they want — what they describe wanting)

---

## Source Tier 2: Good Signal

### Twitter/X & LinkedIn
- Search for complaints about the category problem
- Look for "anyone know a tool that does X" threads
- Find influencer posts about the topic — read the comment section

### YouTube Comments
- On tutorial videos in your category
- On "Day in the life" videos for your ICP
- On competitor demo videos (what are people asking for?)

### Competitor Landing Pages
- What pain do they lead with?
- What outcome do they promise?
- What objections do they pre-empt?
- Use this to find gaps they're not addressing

---

## What to Extract

For each source, capture:

| Type | What It Looks Like | How to Use It |
|---|---|---|
| Pain language | "I was drowning in..." / "It was such a mess" | Problem section copy, headline empathy |
| Outcome language | "I finally feel like..." / "Now I can actually..." | Hero headline, benefit bullets |
| Before/after | "Before [X] I used to... Now I..." | Testimonial format, case study framing |
| Objections | "I was worried it would..." / "At first I thought..." | FAQ, objection handling section |
| Comparison language | "Better than [X] because..." | Competitor comparison pages |
| Trigger events | "When [thing happened], I decided..." | Ad targeting, email subject lines |

## How to Use VOC in Copy

### Direct Lift (with attribution)
Pull the exact phrase as a testimonial quote. E.g.:
> "I went from spending 3 hours on reports to 15 minutes." — Sarah K., Marketing Manager

### Paraphrase Into Headlines
Customer says: *"I was always worried I'd miss something important in my inbox."*
Headline becomes: *"Never miss a critical message again."*

### Mirror in Body Copy
Customer says: *"It was like trying to juggle 10 things at once."*
Body copy: *"Stop juggling. [Product] brings everything into one place."*

### FAQ From Objections
Customer says: *"I wasn't sure if it would work for a team my size."*
FAQ becomes: *"Is this right for small teams?"*

## VOC Extraction Template

When pulling research, organise into this format:

```
SOURCE: [G2 / Reddit / Sales call / etc.]
DATE PULLED: [date]

PAIN PHRASES:
- "..."
- "..."

OUTCOME PHRASES:
- "..."
- "..."

OBJECTIONS:
- "..."
- "..."

TRIGGER EVENTS:
- "..."

COMPETITOR MENTIONS:
- "..."
```

## Anti-Patterns

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Only reading 5-star reviews | Too generic and positive to be useful | Focus on 3–4 star for nuance |
| Paraphrasing everything | Loses the authentic voice | Lift exact phrases where possible |
| Skipping competitor reviews | Misses the biggest opportunity gap | Always mine competitor 1–2 star reviews |
| Not tagging by type | Mixes pain/outcome/objection — hard to use | Categorise as you collect |
| Collecting but not applying | Research sitting in a doc unused | Map each insight to a specific copy element |

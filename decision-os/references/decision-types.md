# Decision Types — Default Criteria Sets

Use this file in Stage 0 to classify the decision and load the appropriate
default criteria for Stage 1 (Weighted Decision Matrix).

Always customise the criteria based on what the user actually said matters.
These are starting points, not rigid templates.

---

## Career Decisions

**Triggers:** Job offer, promotion, career switch, staying vs leaving, role change

**Default criteria:**
| Criterion | Default Weight | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| Compensation (total, not just base) | 0.15 | Include equity, benefits, bonus |
| Growth trajectory | 0.20 | Where does this role lead in 3–5 years? |
| Skill development | 0.20 | What new skills / leverage do you build? |
| Mission / meaning | 0.15 | Do you care about what the org does? |
| Work quality & autonomy | 0.15 | How much do you control your work? |
| People & culture | 0.10 | Who are you building relationships with? |
| Stability & risk | 0.05 | Job security, company stage |

**Key questions to ask:**
- "What does the ceiling look like in each option — not just the floor?"
- "Who specifically would you be learning from?"
- "What's the realistic comp trajectory 3 years in?"

## Entrepreneurship / Startup

**Triggers:** Start a company, quit to build, join as co-founder, full-time vs side project

**Default criteria:**
| Criterion | Default Weight | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| Market size & timing | 0.20 | Is this a real problem at real scale? |
| Founder-market fit | 0.20 | Are you the right person for this? |
| Financial runway | 0.15 | How long can you survive without revenue? |
| Idea conviction | 0.15 | Obsession score 1–10 |
| Downside if it fails | 0.15 | Reputational, financial, relationship cost |
| Timing / opportunity window | 0.15 | Will this be easier or harder later? |

**Key second-order traps:**
- "Building the product" becomes "running the company" fast — are you excited about both?
- Fundraising consumes 6+ months of focus; often misses product window
- Co-founder dynamics are the #1 early-stage failure mode

## Education / MBA

**Triggers:** MBA, grad school, bootcamp, certification, further study

**Default criteria:**
| Criterion | Default Weight | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| ROI on total cost (time + money) | 0.25 | True financial payback period |
| Network quality | 0.20 | Who you'll meet and stay connected to |
| Career optionality unlocked | 0.20 | Doors that open vs doors that close |
| Skill gap it fills | 0.15 | Do you actually need this credential? |
| Opportunity cost | 0.20 | What 2 years of alternative path produces |

**Key questions:**
- "What specifically are you going to an MBA to get that you can't get another way?"
- "Name 3 people from that programme who are doing exactly what you want to do."
- "Have you talked to people 5 years out who did it — not 1 year out?"

**Common MBA trap:**
The decision feels like "investment in myself" which makes the cost feel
more palatable than it is. Always calculate the true cost:
Tuition + living costs + foregone salary + foregone equity = often $300–500k+
for top programmes.

## Financial Decisions

**Triggers:** Investment, major purchase, financial restructuring, real estate

**Default criteria:**
| Criterion | Default Weight | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| Expected return | 0.20 | Realistic, not optimistic case |
| Downside / loss scenario | 0.25 | Can you survive the worst case? |
| Liquidity impact | 0.15 | How does this affect cash flexibility? |
| Timeline fit | 0.15 | Does return horizon match your need? |
| Opportunity cost of capital | 0.15 | What else could this money do? |
| Stress / complexity cost | 0.10 | How much ongoing attention does it need? |

**Hard rule:**
Never recommend a financial decision that puts someone in an irreversible
negative position (bankruptcy, unserviceable debt, loss of primary residence).
Flag this as a catastrophe scenario always.

## Geographic / Relocation

**Triggers:** Move city, move country, remote vs in-person, base location

**Default criteria:**
| Criterion | Default Weight | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| Career opportunity density | 0.20 | Quality of professional opportunities |
| Cost of living vs income | 0.15 | Real purchasing power, not just headline salary |
| Social / relationship impact | 0.25 | Existing relationships affected |
| Lifestyle fit | 0.15 | Climate, culture, pace, activities |
| Long-term rootedness | 0.10 | Do you want to be here in 10 years? |
| Family / partner alignment | 0.15 | Is this decision shared or unilateral? |

**Most underweighted factor:**
Social network rebuild. Moving cities resets your social graph.
Research suggests it takes 2–3 years to build meaningful friendships in a new city.
This has measurable effects on mental health and wellbeing — it's not trivial.

## Build vs Buy / Make vs Outsource

**Triggers:** Business decisions about building internal capability vs purchasing

**Default criteria:**
| Criterion | Default Weight | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| Core competency alignment | 0.25 | Is this what you should be great at? |
| Total cost of ownership | 0.20 | Build: hidden ongoing maintenance cost |
| Time to value | 0.15 | Buy is usually faster to deploy |
| Control & customisation | 0.20 | Build = more control, more commitment |
| Vendor risk | 0.10 | What if they change pricing / fold? |
| Team capability | 0.10 | Do you have the people to build + maintain? |

**Key principle:**
Buy for commodity functions; build for differentiators.
The question is: "Does excellence in this function directly create value for customers?"
If yes → strong case to build or own deeply. If no → buy.

## Relationship / Personal Decisions

**Triggers:** Major relationship change, commitment, living together, long-distance

**Default criteria (adapt carefully — these are deeply personal):**
| Criterion | Default Weight | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| Alignment on core values | 0.25 | Not interests — fundamental values |
| Communication quality | 0.20 | How do you handle conflict? |
| Life direction compatibility | 0.20 | Where are you each going? |
| Emotional experience | 0.20 | How do you feel most of the time with them? |
| Growth facilitation | 0.15 | Do they make you more of who you want to be? |

**Note for relationship decisions:**
Be careful with quantitative frameworks here. A relationship scoring 8.5 vs 7.9
tells you almost nothing. Lean harder on regret minimisation (Stage 5) and
identity alignment (Stage 6) for these decisions.

## Tone & Depth Calibration by Stakes

| Stakes Level | Depth | Tone |
|-------------|-------|------|
| Life-defining, irreversible | Run all 9 stages in full | Measured, thorough, honest |
| High stakes, costly to reverse | Run all 9, compress stages 6–7 | Direct, warm |
| Medium stakes, reversible | Run stages 1, 3, 5, 9 | Efficient, practical |
| Low stakes, easily reversible | Note this, run stage 1 + 9 only | Light, encourage experimentation |

For low-stakes reversible decisions, say: "This is actually a cheap experiment.
Pick one, run it for [X months], and use the data to decide for real."

# Voice Styles

Use this to calibrate *how* a framework gets written, once you know *what* personality the brand/subject has. These are starting points, not rigid boxes — real brands often blend two.

## Premium Minimalist
- Short, declarative sentences. Lots of implied white space even in plain text.
- Verb-first, outcome-first. Avoids exclamation points and emoji almost entirely.
- Sounds like: "Fuel the work that matters." / "Built for focus."

## Warm / Community
- Second-person, conversational, occasional rhetorical question.
- Names small relatable moments rather than abstract values.
- Sounds like: "You know that feeling when the first sip just... fixes everything?"

## Bold / Disruptive
- Sentence fragments, contrast, occasional rule-breaking grammar for rhythm.
- Comfortable naming what's broken about the status quo before naming the fix.
- Sounds like: "Most apps want your attention. This one wants you to put it down."

## Luxury / Editorial
- Longer, more textured sentences; precise, sensory word choice.
- Confidence without exclamation — luxury rarely shouts.
- Sounds like: "Cut for the version of yourself that doesn't apologize for ambition."

## Playful / Gen-Z
- Casual punctuation, internet-native rhythm, self-aware humor.
- Comfortable being a little silly in service of being memorable.
- Sounds like: "POV: it's 7am and your coffee believes in you more than you do."

## How to pick

If the user gave personality adjectives, map them directly: "warm, ambitious, minimalist" → blend Premium Minimalist structure with Warm word choice. If no adjectives were given, infer from the subject itself (a B2B fintech tool skews Premium Minimalist by default; a youth fitness app skews Playful/Bold) and state the assumption in one line before delivering output.

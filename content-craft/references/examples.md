# Worked Examples

These show the full before → after across three different subjects (not just marketing/ads), to demonstrate this skill works on any content brief, not a narrow ad-copy use case.

---

## Example 1: Specialty coffee brand (tagline + caption + video script)

**Thin request:** "Promote our coffee shop."

**Filled brief (BAEO):** Brand = warm, ambitious, minimalist. Audience = remote workers, 25-40. Emotion = belonging + quiet productivity. Outcome = feel this brand understands their daily ritual.

**Taglines (3 variants):**
- *Clear:* "Where Great Ideas Start Brewing."
- *Premium:* "Fuel the Work That Matters."
- *Warm:* "Your First Win of the Day."

**Caption:** see `frameworks.md#caption` — the full worked example lives there.

**Video script:** see `frameworks.md#video-script`.

## Example 2: SaaS product update (email subject + LinkedIn post)

**Thin request:** "Write something about our new export feature."

**Filled brief (BAEO):** Brand = practical, no-nonsense B2B tool. Audience = ops managers who filed complaints about the old export flow. Emotion = relief ("finally"). Outcome = open the email / try the feature.

**Email subject (3 variants):**
- *Specific:* "We rebuilt the export flow you complained about"
- *Curiosity + specific:* "The export bug from March? Fixed."
- *Outcome-first:* "Export 10,000 rows in under a second now"

**LinkedIn post (Hook → Insight → Story → Lesson → CTA):**
> Our most-requested feature wasn't a feature. It was an apology.
>
> For eight months, exporting more than 5,000 rows in [Product] meant a coffee break while you waited — or a support ticket when it timed out.
>
> We didn't add export speed as a "nice to have." We rebuilt the underlying query engine because customers were quietly working around us, not with us — building their own scripts just to get their own data out.
>
> The lesson: sometimes the most important roadmap item is the one customers stopped asking for, because they'd already given up on you fixing it.
>
> If you hit that wall with us before — try it again this week. Tell us what breaks next.

## Example 3: Fitness app founder story (About page)

**Thin request:** "Write our About page."

**Filled brief (BAEO):** Brand = bold, no-excuses, but not aggressive. Audience = people who've quit fitness apps before. Emotion = pride in showing up. Outcome = trust that this app is built differently.

**Founder story (Before → Struggle → Insight → Transformation → Invitation):**
> Before this app existed, I had eleven fitness apps on my phone and zero habits to show for it.
>
> Every one of them wanted forty-five minutes, a gym membership, and a version of me that had way more free time than I actually did. So I quit. All eleven times.
>
> The insight came on a Tuesday when I did seven minutes of squats in my kitchen because that's genuinely all the time I had — and felt better about that than any "perfect" workout I'd skipped.
>
> That's the only feature this app is built around: the workout that actually happens beats the workout that's theoretically perfect. Twenty minutes, no equipment, no excuses engineered out of the plan before you even start.
>
> If you've quit eleven apps too — this one's built for the version of you that shows up for seven minutes on a Tuesday, not the one that has a perfect morning routine. Start there.

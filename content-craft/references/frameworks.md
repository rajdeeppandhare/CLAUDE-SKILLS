# Content Frameworks

Each section below is a self-contained pattern: what it's for, the structural formula, and a worked example. Read only the section(s) relevant to the current request.

---

## Tagline

**For:** taglines, slogans, one-liners, hero headlines.

**Formula:** `Brand personality + Audience truth + Emotion + Outcome`, compressed into 3-8 words. The trap is writing a *description* ("Fresh Coffee Every Day") instead of a *promise* ("Fuel the Work That Matters"). A good tagline implies the brand without naming the product category.

**Escalation ladder** (use to show range across variants):
- Weak (describes the product): "Fresh Coffee Every Day"
- Stronger (implies a feeling): "Where Great Ideas Start Brewing"
- Premium (claims an identity): "Fuel the Work That Matters"

**Worked example — fitness app, audience = busy parents, emotion = pride, outcome = consistency:**
- Weak: "Workouts That Fit Your Schedule"
- Stronger: "Twenty Minutes. Every Excuse Covered."
- Premium: "Strong Enough to Show Up Tomorrow."

## Caption

**For:** social captions, post copy, short-form written content.

**Formula:** `Hook → Emotion → Benefit → CTA`
- **Hook** (line 1): a sharp, specific claim or question — never a greeting or generic opener.
- **Emotion**: 1-2 lines that locate the feeling, often by naming a relatable moment.
- **Benefit**: what the product/idea actually delivers, stated concretely, not abstractly.
- **CTA**: a low-friction question or invitation, not a hard sell.

**Worked example:**
> Your morning coffee isn't just a drink.
> It's the first promise you make to yourself.
> A quiet moment before the meetings, notifications, and deadlines begin.
> Brewed from ethically sourced beans and crafted for people building something meaningful.
> What's your morning ritual? ☕

## Video Script

**For:** short ad scripts, reels, 15-60 second video voiceovers.

**Formula:** `Problem (relatable friction) → Ritual/Shift (the product enters naturally) → Payoff (visible change) → Brand line (one sentence, logo beat)`. Keep each beat to one visual + one VO line. Resist explaining the product — show the *before/after* of the person, not a feature list.

**Worked example:**
```
[Scene: Someone staring at a laptop, struggling to focus.]
VO: Some mornings, motivation doesn't show up on time.

[Coffee pours slowly.]
VO: That's why rituals matter.

[Person takes a sip and starts working confidently.]
VO: Small moments create momentum.

[Brand logo appears.]
VO: Start your day with purpose. [Brand Name]
    Fuel the work that matters.
```

**Generalizes beyond ads:** the same Problem → Shift → Payoff → Line shape works for app onboarding videos, internal culture videos, and founder intro reels — swap "product enters" for "feature/decision enters."

## Blog Hook

**For:** article openings, landing page hero copy, the first 1-2 sentences anyone reads before deciding to keep going.

**Three reliable openings — pick based on tone:**
1. **Open loop**: state a tension without resolving it yet. *"Most teams don't have a productivity problem. They have a decision problem."*
2. **Contrarian claim**: state the opposite of common wisdom, then earn it. *"Cold brew isn't smoother because it's cold. It's smoother because it's wrong on purpose."*
3. **Specific stat/detail**: one concrete, slightly surprising number or fact, not a vague statistic. *"The average remote worker checks Slack 47 times before lunch."*

Avoid opening with a question the reader already knows the answer to ("Ever wonder why...") — it reads as filler.

## Email Subject

**For:** email subject lines.

**Formula:** specificity beats cleverness almost every time. A subject line that names a concrete detail ("Your draft is missing one section") outperforms a punny or vague one ("Don't miss this!") in almost every real-world test. Use a curiosity gap only when you can also be concrete — name *what* is being teased, not just *that* something exists.

**Worked example (SaaS product update):**
- Weak: "Big News Inside! 🎉"
- Stronger: "We rebuilt the export flow you complained about"

## LinkedIn / Thought Leadership

**For:** LinkedIn posts, founder updates, professional narrative posts.

**Formula:** `Hook (1 line, no preamble) → Insight (the non-obvious claim) → Story (a short, specific moment that proves the insight) → Lesson (what the reader should take away) → CTA (a question or soft invitation, not "link in bio")`. The story beat is what separates this from generic advice-posting — specificity ("we lost our biggest client over a Slack message") beats generality ("communication matters") every time.

---

## Founder Story

**For:** About pages, brand origin content, founder-story posts.

**Formula:** `Before (the ordinary state) → Struggle (the specific frustration that started it) → Insight (the moment something clicked) → Transformation (what changed because of it) → Invitation (how the reader/customer fits into that story now)`. Keep "Before" and "Struggle" concrete and small-scale (a specific bad day, not "the industry was broken") — grand claims about the industry read as marketing; a specific bad Tuesday reads as true.

## Minimalist Voice (Apple/Nike-style)

**For:** any request to sound more premium, minimal, or "Apple/Nike-like."

**Principles, not just vocabulary:**
- Short, declarative sentences. Cut connecting words ("and," "because," "in order to") wherever the meaning survives without them.
- Verb-first CTAs: "Start," "Build," "Create" — not "You can start" or "Why not start."
- State the outcome, not the mechanism. Apple says "Shoot in 4K," not "Our advanced sensor technology enables 4K capture."
- One idea per sentence, one emotion per paragraph. Premium copy has space around it — resist the urge to add a second clause that explains the first.
- Abstraction over feature-listing: the emotional claim ("Fuel the work that matters") sits above the feature ("ethically sourced beans") — lead with the former, let the latter support it in smaller text/lower in the caption.

## India — GST {#india}

### Rate Structure
| Rate | Category |
|---|---|
| 0% | Essential food items, grains, milk, eggs, fresh vegetables |
| 5% | Packaged food, coal, household items, economy class travel |
| 12% | Computers, processed food, business class travel |
| 18% | Most services, electronics, capital goods |
| 28% | Luxury goods, automobiles, tobacco, aerated drinks |
| 28% + Cess | Sin goods (tobacco, luxury cars) |

### Return Types
| Return | Who Files | Frequency | Due Date |
|---|---|---|---|
| GSTR-1 | Regular taxpayers (outward supplies) | Monthly / Quarterly | 11th / 13th of next month |
| GSTR-3B | All regular taxpayers (summary) | Monthly | 20th of next month |
| GSTR-9 | Annual return | Annual | 31 December |
| GSTR-9C | Reconciliation statement (turnover > ₹5Cr) | Annual | 31 December |
| CMP-08 | Composition dealers | Quarterly | 18th of next month |

### Input Tax Credit (ITC) Rules
- ITC available on business purchases (not personal use)
- Blocked credits: motor vehicles (exceptions apply), food & beverages, club memberships, personal expenses
- ITC reversal required if payment not made within 180 days
- Proportionate reversal for exempt supplies

### Threshold
- Mandatory registration: ₹40L turnover (goods); ₹20L (services)
- Special category states: ₹10L threshold
- Composition scheme: up to ₹1.5Cr (goods); ₹50L (services)

### E-Invoicing
- Mandatory for turnover > ₹5Cr (as of August 2023)
- IRN generation required before issuing invoice to B2B customers

## Australia — GST {#australia}

### Key Facts
- Rate: 10% flat
- Registration threshold: AUD $75,000 turnover (AUD $150,000 for non-profits)
- Reporting: via Business Activity Statement (BAS)
- Frequency: monthly, quarterly, or annual

### GST-Free vs Input-Taxed vs Taxable
| Category | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Basic food, exports, medical | GST-free (no GST, full ITC) |
| Residential rent, financial supplies | Input-taxed (no GST, reduced ITC) |
| Standard business supplies | Taxable (10% GST, full ITC) |

### BAS Lodgement
- Quarterly due: 28 days after quarter end (or 28 Feb for Q2)
- Monthly due: 21st of following month
- Annual due: 31 October

## UK — VAT {#uk}

### Rate Structure
| Rate | Category |
|---|---|
| 20% | Standard (most goods and services) |
| 5% | Domestic fuel, energy-saving materials, children's car seats |
| 0% | Food, children's clothing, books, public transport, exports |
| Exempt | Financial services, insurance, education, health |

### Registration
- Mandatory threshold: £90,000 (2024/25)
- Voluntary registration allowed below threshold
- Deregistration threshold: £88,000

### Returns & Payment
- Standard: quarterly VAT return + payment within 1 month + 7 days
- Annual Accounting Scheme: one return/year, monthly/quarterly advance payments
- Making Tax Digital (MTD): mandatory for all VAT-registered businesses

### Key Concepts
- Flat Rate Scheme: fixed % of gross turnover (for small businesses < £150K)
- Cash Accounting: VAT based on cash received/paid (< £1.35M turnover)
- Margin Scheme: for second-hand goods dealers

## EU — VAT {#eu}

### General
- VAT rates vary by country (standard 17–27%)
- One Stop Shop (OSS): for digital services to EU consumers — register once, file one return
- Reverse Charge: B2B cross-border services — buyer accounts for VAT
- Intrastat: statistical reporting for goods between EU member states
- EU VAT threshold for distance selling: €10,000 (then destination country rates apply)

### Common Standard Rates
| Country | Standard Rate |
|---|---|
| Germany | 19% |
| France | 20% |
| Netherlands | 21% |
| Ireland | 23% |
| Italy | 22% |
| Spain | 21% |
| Sweden | 25% |
| Luxembourg | 17% (lowest in EU) |
| Hungary | 27% (highest in EU) |

## UAE — VAT {#uae}

### Key Facts
- Rate: 5% flat
- Mandatory registration: AED 375,000 taxable turnover
- Voluntary: AED 187,500
- Exempt: residential real estate, local transport, certain financial services
- Zero-rated: exports, international transport, healthcare, education

### Return Filing
- Frequency: quarterly (most) or monthly (large businesses)
- Due date: 28th of month following tax period
- Filed via FTA (Federal Tax Authority) portal

## Canada — GST/HST {#canada}

### Rate Structure
| Province | GST | HST | PST | Total |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ontario | — | 13% | — | 13% |
| BC | 5% | — | 7% | 12% |
| Alberta | 5% | — | — | 5% |
| Quebec | 5% | — | 9.975% | 14.975% |
| Nova Scotia | — | 15% | — | 15% |
| Manitoba | 5% | — | 7% | 12% |

### Registration
- Mandatory: CAD $30,000 taxable supplies in 4 consecutive calendar quarters
- Small supplier exemption below threshold

### Filing Frequency
- Annual: < $1.5M
- Quarterly: $1.5M–$6M
- Monthly: > $6M

## Singapore — GST {#singapore}

### Key Facts
- Rate: 9% (increased from 8% on 1 Jan 2024)
- Registration threshold: SGD $1M taxable turnover
- Exempt: financial services, residential property rental, sale of investment precious metals
- Zero-rated: exports, international services

### Returns
- Quarterly GST F5 returns
- Due: one month after end of accounting period
- Reverse charge applies for imported services (B2B)

## USA — Sales Tax {#usa}

### Overview
- No federal sales tax — each state sets its own rate
- 45 states + DC have sales tax; 5 states don't (Alaska, Delaware, Montana, New Hampshire, Oregon)
- Economic nexus triggered by $100,000 in sales or 200 transactions in most states

### Common State Rates (2024)
| State | State Rate | With Local | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| California | 7.25% | Up to 10.75% | Highest base rate |
| New York | 4% | Up to 8.875% | NYC 8.875% |
| Texas | 6.25% | Up to 8.25% | |
| Florida | 6% | Up to 8.5% | |
| Washington | 6.5% | Up to 10.6% | |
| Tennessee | 7% | Up to 9.75% | High combined rate |

### Marketplace Facilitator Laws
- Amazon, Etsy, eBay, etc. collect and remit sales tax on behalf of sellers in most states
- Sellers may still have filing obligations in some states

## Cross-Border Digital Services {#digital}

### Key Rule: Tax Where the Customer Is
Most jurisdictions now tax digital services at the customer's location:

| Jurisdiction | Rule |
|---|---|
| EU | VAT via OSS registration or local registration |
| UK | VAT at 20%, registration if > £90K UK sales |
| Australia | GST if > AUD $75K to Australian consumers |
| India | 18% GST on OIDAR services; foreign providers must register |
| Singapore | GST if > SGD $1M global + any Singapore sales |
| Canada | GST/HST if > CAD $30K to Canadian consumers |
| UAE | 5% VAT on digital services to UAE residents |
| USA | Economic nexus rules apply state by state |

**SaaS / Software**: Generally taxable in most jurisdictions
**Consulting / Professional Services**: Rules vary — often taxable in customer's country under B2C; reverse charge under B2B
